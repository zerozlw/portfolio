## AI Products Are Different

Traditional software does exactly what you tell it. AI products do what they think you want. This fundamental difference changes everything.

## The Probabilistic Mindset

With traditional software:
- Input A → Output B (always)
- Bugs are deterministic
- Testing is straightforward

With AI products:
- Input A → Output B (usually)
- "Bugs" are probabilistic
- Testing requires statistical thinking

## Design Implications

### 1. Embrace Uncertainty

Don't fight it — design for it:

```
Instead of: "The answer is X"
Try: "Based on the available information, X seems most likely.
      Here's my confidence level and reasoning."
```

### 2. Feedback Loops Are Everything

AI products improve with usage. Design for feedback from day one:

- Was this helpful? 👍/👎
- Was this accurate?
- What were you actually looking for?

### 3. Graceful Degradation

When the AI isn't sure, it should:
1. Say so clearly
2. Offer alternatives
3. Escalate to humans when needed

### 4. Measure What Matters

Traditional metrics (uptime, latency) still apply, but add:
- **Accuracy** — Is the output correct?
- **Usefulness** — Did it help the user?
- **Trust** — Does the user believe the output?

## The Product Manager's Role

In AI products, the PM's job is to:
1. **Set boundaries** — What should the AI try? What should it avoid?
2. **Design guardrails** — Safety rails that don't kill UX
3. **Define success** — What does "good enough" look like?
4. **Iterate fast** — AI products need rapid experimentation

## Conclusion

AI product thinking is about embracing uncertainty while building trust. It's harder than traditional product work, but also more rewarding.
