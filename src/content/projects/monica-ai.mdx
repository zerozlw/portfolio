## Background

Monica is an AI browser extension targeting **overseas users**, bringing ChatGPT-level capabilities directly into the browser — chat, quick actions, reading assistant, ChatPDF, YouTube summary, and more. As the product grew more complex, a pattern emerged: **most users only scratched the surface**. They used Chat and Quick Actions, ignored everything else, and had no reason to upgrade to paid plans.

Meanwhile, user feedback was flowing in daily through email — feature requests, pain points, and usage questions. These signals were scattered and unstructured, making it hard to prioritize what to build next.

The core challenge: **understand our overseas users deeply enough to make product decisions that actually move the needle on retention and conversion**.

</En>

## 背景

Monica 是一款面向**海外用户**的 AI 浏览器插件，将 ChatGPT 级别的能力直接嵌入浏览器——Chat、快捷操作、阅读助手、ChatPDF、YouTube 摘要等。随着产品功能日益复杂，一个现象浮现：**大部分用户只用了皮毛**。他们用 Chat 和 Quick Action，忽略其他功能，也没有升级付费的意愿。

与此同时，用户反馈每天从邮箱涌入——功能请求、使用痛点、使用疑问。这些信号分散且无结构，很难判断优先级。

核心挑战：**深入理解海外用户，做出真正能提升留存和转化的产品决策**。

</Zh>

## What I Did

**User Research.** Participated in the full research cycle, combining **survey data** (20,000 responses) with **daily email feedback** for comprehensive analysis.

We ran two parallel research tracks:

- **Email feedback**: Collected daily user feedback from inbox, replied to users, followed up on issues, and identified recurring patterns. When a problem was mentioned repeatedly, it became a priority.
- **Overseas interviews**: Semi-structured interviews with high-frequency users across different countries and regions, confirming paid vs. free user motivations and pain points.

Through cross-validation of both methods, we determined which features were worth optimizing, used competitive analysis to shape the approach, and measured results through feedback data and retention rates.

**What we learned from users.** The research revealed clear patterns across user groups:

> *"I use Monica every day for work emails and document polishing. The sidebar is convenient — I don't need to open a new tab like ChatGPT."* — Unpaid user, UX Designer

> *"40 free queries are enough for me. I'm a student, I can't afford paid plans."* — Unpaid user, Student

> *"I subscribed for YouTube Summary. Before watching a video, I check the summary to see if it's worth my time."* — Paid user, AI Engineer, $240K+ income

> *"I used Monica for my master's thesis — reading 20-60 page papers with ChatPDF. But sometimes the PDF uploads but Monica can't read it."* — Paid user, Special Education Teacher

**Three user groups emerged, each with distinct needs:**

**Group 1: Free users who don't explore (7/9 interviewees).** They used only Chat and Quick Actions, never discovered other features. Some didn't even know PDF reader or YouTube summary existed. They were satisfied with basics and had no motivation to upgrade.
→ Optimized the **Quick Toolbar** to surface high-value features upfront.

**Group 2: Non-native English speakers (9/11 interviewees).** They used Monica primarily for email and document polishing — a workflow we hadn't specifically optimized for.
→ Enhanced **Language Translation** to better serve their document polishing workflow.

**Group 3: Students needing academic tools (3/9 interviewees).** They needed to read 20-60 page papers, compare multiple PDFs, and preserve images during translation — features ChatPDF didn't handle well.
→ Optimized **PDF Reader (ChatPDF)** for academic use cases: multi-PDF comparison and image-preserving translation.

**Some requests we chose not to pursue.** Users asked for many features, but not all were worth building. For example, several users wanted vibe coding capabilities or adding niche languages to translation. Given cost, ROI, and engineering bandwidth, we deprioritized these — a reminder that saying no is as important as saying yes.

## 我的行动

**用户研究。** 参与了完整的调研流程，将**调研数据**与**邮箱反馈数据**结合分析。

我们同时推进两条调研线：

- **邮箱反馈**：每日定时挖掘有价值的邮箱反馈，给予反馈并追问用户。如果一个问题被反复提及，就会形成"这个问题相对重要"的结论。
- **海外访谈**：抽取高频用户中不同国家和地区的用户进行半结构化访谈，确认付费用户和免费用户的使用动机和痛点。

通过两种调研方法交叉验证，我们向上层说明哪些功能值得推进优化，并通过竞品分析确认优化的形态和方向，最后通过反馈数据及留存率来判断方案结果，形成闭环。

<div className="not-prose my-6">
<img src="/images/projects/monica-research-zh.svg" alt="调研流程" className="w-full rounded-xl bg-white/70" />

**用户说了什么。** 调研揭示了不同用户群体的清晰画像：

> *"我每天用 Monica 处理工作邮件和润色文档。侧边栏很方便——不需要像 ChatGPT 那样开新标签页。"* — 免费用户，UX 设计师

> *"40 个免费 query 对我够用了。我是学生，买不起付费版。"* — 免费用户，学生

> *"我订阅是为了 YouTube Summary。看视频前先看摘要，判断值不值得花时间。"* — 付费用户，AI 工程师，收入 $240K+

> *"我用 Monica 写硕士论文——读 20-60 页的课程文档。但有时候 PDF 上传成功了 Monica 却读不出来。"* — 付费用户，特殊教育老师

**三类用户群体浮现，每类需求不同：**

**群体 1：不探索功能的免费用户（7/9 受访者）。** 他们只用 Chat 和 Quick Actions，从未发现其他功能。部分用户甚至不知道 PDF 阅读和 YouTube 摘要的存在。他们满足于基础功能，没有升级动力。
→ 优化**快捷工具栏**，将高价值功能前置展示。

**群体 2：非英语母语用户（9/11 受访者）。** 他们主要用 Monica 润色邮件和文档——这是一个我们未专门优化的工作流。
→ 增强**语言翻译**能力，更好地服务文档润色场景。

**群体 3：需要学术工具的学生（3/9 受访者）。** 他们需要阅读 20-60 页论文、对比多个 PDF、翻译时保留图片——ChatPDF 之前做不好这些。
→ 优化 **PDF 阅读（ChatPDF）**，支持多 PDF 对比和保留图片的翻译。

**有些需求我们选择不做。** 用户提了很多需求，但不是每个都值得做。比如有用户希望增加 vibe coding 能力，或者翻译新增小语种。结合成本、投入产出比和工程资源，我们选择不优先推进——**说不做和说做一样重要**。
